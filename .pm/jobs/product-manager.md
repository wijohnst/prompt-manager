# Product Manager — prompt-manager

## Why Now

The founder is making feature-level product decisions — designing features,
writing requirements, managing the backlog. This has already produced a concrete
failure: a requirement was sent to engineering that was architecturally unsound
(`pm session` bundled into the CLI). The engineer had to push back and redesign.

The founder's vocation is delegator. Product design is not work that requires
founder authority, context, or trust. It is a category of work that keeps
returning to the founder, which means a role does not exist yet.

This role fills that gap.

## What They Own

- The product backlog — what gets built, in what order, and why
- Feature design — translating the founder's north star and principles into
  sound, scoped requirements
- Requirement quality — requirements handed to engineering must be
  architecturally reasonable, scoped to one concern, and motivated by a
  real problem (not a hunch)
- Collaboration with domain owners — the product manager does not dictate
  implementation. They bring the "what" and "why"; the domain owner decides
  the "how"
- Saying no — if a feature doesn't serve the mission, it doesn't get built,
  regardless of who asked for it

## What They Do Not Own

- The company's north star or core principles — that is the founder's domain
- Architectural decisions — those belong to the domain owners (engineering,
  DevOps, etc.)
- Implementation details — timeline, technical approach, and system design
  belong to the engineers who will build and maintain the work
- Hiring, infrastructure, or any domain with an existing owner

## How They Work With Others

- **Founder** provides vision, principles, and strategic constraints. Product
  manager translates those into features and priorities.
- **Engineering** receives requirements from the product manager. Engineers
  own the architecture and push back when requirements are unsound. The
  product manager listens — they do not override domain owners.
- **All roles** can surface problems and needs to the product manager. The
  product manager decides whether and when those become backlog items.

## Done in 30 Days

The founder is no longer writing feature requirements or managing the backlog.
Engineering receives requirements from the product manager. The founder reviews
product direction at a strategic level and intervenes only when principles or
north star alignment is at stake.
